## Rubric

Use this project rubric to understand and assess the project criteria.

## Communication and Presentation

| Criteria | Submission Requirements |
| --- | --- |
| Presentation Structure and Clarity | The 15-minute presentation is clearly structured and follows a logical flow. The student explains the industry problem, system design, integration, evaluation, and ethical considerations in a way that is understandable to both technical and non-technical audiences. |
| Professional Communication | The student communicates confidently and professionally in English. Explanations are clear, concise, and organized. Visual aids (slides or diagrams) support understanding rather than distract from it. |
| Time Management | The presentation is planned and delivered within the 15-minute time limit without rushing or omitting required sections. |

## Technical Understanding and Integration

| Criteria | Submission Requirements |
| --- | --- |
| System Explanation | The student accurately explains the integrated AI system or case study developed in Project 7, including how major components interact and what the system is intended to do. |
| Integration Across Capstone Projects | The student clearly explains how at least three prior capstone projects were integrated. Each project’s contribution is accurately described, and the rationale for integration is sound and coherent. |
| Technical Decision Justification | Key technical decisions are explained logically. The student demonstrates understanding of tradeoffs, limitations, and alternatives, rather than simply describing what was built. |

## Ethical and Responsible AI Reasoning

| Criteria | Submission Requirements |
| --- | --- |
| Ethical Awareness | The student identifies ethical risks relevant to the industry and system, such as bias, misuse, transparency, accountability, or safety concerns. |
| Ethical Reasoning and Safeguards | Ethical considerations are tied directly to the student’s system design and behavior. The student explains how ethical risks influenced decisions or safeguards, rather than providing generic statements. |

## Evaluation, Reflection, and Judgment

| Criteria | Submission Requirements |
| --- | --- |
| Evaluation of System Behavior | The student accurately discusses how the system was evaluated, including strengths, limitations, failure cases, or risks observed during testing or analysis. |
| Reflective Judgment |  |

## Defense and Mentor Interaction

| Criteria | Submission Requirements |
| --- | --- |
| Responsiveness During Defense | The student responds thoughtfully to mentor questions, demonstrating understanding of their work. Answers are grounded in the student’s actual implementation, analysis, and paper. |
| Professional Readiness | The student demonstrates readiness for real-world AI roles through clear reasoning, ethical awareness, and the ability to explain complex work to others. |
