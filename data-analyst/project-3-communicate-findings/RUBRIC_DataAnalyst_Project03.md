## Exploratory Data Analysis

| Criteria | Submission Requirements |
| --- | --- |
| Is the data explored systematically using a series of appropriate and varied visualizations? | Seven (7) visualizations are included in the **exploratory analysis**.  **For each of the categories below, include: 1) Required chart(s) and 2) One additional visualization type.**  ### Univariate Exploration  1. Histogram - required 2. Count Plot  ### Bivariate Exploration  1. Scatterplots - required 2. Box Plots - required 3. Bar Chart 4. Clustered Count Plot 5. Heatmap  ### Multivariate Exploration  1. Facet Plot - required 2. Plot Matrix 3. Scatterplot with multiple encodings 4. Clustered Bar Chart  Plots should include relevant annotations, such as; lines denoting the average value of a distribution or the values in each cell of a cluster plot. ## Explanatory Data Analysis

| Criteria | Submission Requirements |
| --- | --- |
| Does the explanatory data analysis tell a story? | A **separate** explanatory analysis is included. The explanatory analysis presents key insights **taken from** the exploratory analysis - those insights have been **finalized/polished** appropriate for an executive summary of the analysis performed.  The explanatory analysis follows a logical flow and is organized in the following manner:  1. Student presents a clear overarching question or theme that was used to guide their data analysis. 2. Student provides some context for the dataset analyzed. 3. Student presents their findings and key insights. 4. Visualizations from their exploratory analysis are used to support findings. 5. Visualizations should be polished with clear axes, labels, and annotations. |

## Suggestions to Make Your Project Stand Out

- As part of your exploration, fully document your thought processes to justify your steps. Be willing to investigate unexpected relationships, and don’t be afraid of finding a dead-end in your exploration.
- When you select key insights for your explanatory presentation, focus on one or two paths that tell a compelling story.
- When planning your explanation’s flow, document design decisions that make your visualizations information-rich but still easy to read.
- Gather feedback from others to get a different perspective on your explanatory presentation. Document that feedback and note any changes in your designs based on that feedback.
